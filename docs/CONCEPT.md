# STARLINE: LAST SIGNAL - Game Concept Document

## Executive Summary

STARLINE: LAST SIGNAL is a cooperative PvE extraction shooter that combines the best elements of:
- **Helldivers 2** → Galactic war, large-scale operations, stratagems, teamwork
- **Warframe** → Fluid movement, build variety, powerful progression
- **Marathon** → Clean sci-fi aesthetic, mysterious universe, environmental storytelling
- **ULTRAKILL** → Fast, aggressive movement and skill expression
- **Lethal Company** → Resource extraction, risk vs. reward, bringing loot home

## Core Premise

Humanity's civilization exists aboard enormous wandering fleets called **Arks** after the collapse of intergalactic civilization. Three massive galaxies are slowly falling to competing alien civilizations, ancient machines, and living cosmic horrors.

Players belong to the **Expedition Corps**, elite Drop Troopers whose mission is simple:

1. **Land** on hostile planets
2. **Fight** against five intelligent alien races
3. **Extract** valuable resources
4. **Build** Humanity's Future

Every mission contributes to a living war where every liberated planet permanently changes the universe.

## Three Galaxies

### 1. Genesis Galaxy ★
**The closest galaxy. Mostly reclaimed human colonies.**

- **Difficulty**: 1/5
- **Biome Examples**: Grass Worlds, Frozen Moons, Desert Planets, Ocean Colonies, Jungle Worlds
- **Enemy Strength**: Lowest
- **Purpose**: New player onboarding

### 2. Perseus Reach ★★★
**Mid-game galaxy. Old alien civilizations dominate.**

- **Difficulty**: 3/5
- **Features**: Dyson fragments, Crystal planets, Black hole research stations, Floating continents, Gas giant platforms
- **Enemy Tactics**: Advanced
- **Purpose**: Mid-game challenges and progression

### 3. The Abyss Verge ★★★★★
**The final frontier. Ancient civilizations vanished here. Reality itself becomes unstable.**

- **Difficulty**: 5/5
- **Features**: Living planets, Planets inside stars, Dead galaxies, Artificial worlds, Time fractured environments
- **Audience**: Veteran players only
- **Purpose**: End-game challenges

## Difficulty Scaling

The farther from the center, the stronger enemies become:

```
Center → Genesis → Outer Genesis → Perseus → Outer Perseus → Abyss Verge → Galaxy Edge
```

Each sector increases:
- Enemy intelligence
- Enemy armor
- Enemy abilities
- Environmental hazards
- Boss frequency

## Five Intelligent Alien Races

### 1. The Myr - "The Swarm" 🐛

**Appearance**: Biological insect civilization. Eight-foot warriors with organic armor and hive cities.

**Motivation**: Expand forever. Consume biomass.

**Gameplay**: Huge hordes. Weak individually. Dangerous together.

**Planet Types**: Jungles, Caves, Wetlands

### 2. The Khor Dominion - "The Empire" 🦖

**Appearance**: Heavy reptilian soldiers with military culture, advanced armor, and industrial cities.

**Motivation**: Conquer all civilizations.

**Gameplay**: Disciplined squads. Tanks. Dropships. Artillery.

**Planet Types**: Deserts, Volcanic, Fortress Worlds

### 3. The Vey Collective - "The Machines" 🤖

**Appearance**: Elegant white robots with floating drones and synthetic cities.

**Motivation**: Perfect the universe through assimilation.

**Gameplay**: Shield generators. Repair drones. Snipers. Heavy walkers.

**Planet Types**: Artificial worlds, Orbital stations, Megafactories

### 4. The Eclipsed - "The Lost" 👻

**Appearance**: Shadow creatures. Almost invisible. Warp through reality.

**Motivation**: Unknown. Possibly survivors of an extinct civilization.

**Gameplay**: Teleportation. Ambushes. Fear mechanics. Environmental manipulation.

**Planet Types**: Dark planets, Ruins, Ghost worlds

### 5. The Titans - "The Ancients" 🏔️

**Appearance**: 30-foot biomechanical giants. Planet-sized cities. Reality bending technology.

**Motivation**: Protect forbidden technology.

**Gameplay**: Mini-bosses everywhere. Massive enemies. Extremely coordinated AI. Very rare.

## Planet Liberation System

Every planet has an **Occupation %** (Example: 82% Controlled by Myr)

- Players complete operations to reduce enemy control
- When enemy control reaches 0%, the planet becomes **liberated**
- Liberated planets produce resources for humanity
- Entire community contributes to the war effort

## Extraction Gameplay

**Like Lethal Company**, extraction creates tension and meaningful decisions:

- Players physically carry loot
- Large objects require teamwork
- Some resources weigh hundreds of pounds
- Vehicles help transport cargo
- Enemies actively hunt extraction teams
- Limited dropship cargo bay: Do you carry more loot or more ammunition?

## The FieldLink-9

Every Expedition Soldier carries a rugged folding communicator called the **FieldLink-9**. Despite its retro design, it is a hardened quantum communications device that works anywhere humanity has orbital support.

**Key Feature**: Opening it pauses none of the action—the interface is designed for quick one-handed use.

## Operation Deep Dish: Pizza Delivery

A long-running morale program lets soldiers spend earned credits to order hot meals delivered by armored drones.

### Pizza Choices
- Pepperoni
- Supreme
- BBQ Chicken
- Veggie
- Hawaiian (sparking friendly in-universe debates)
- Mystery Slice (rotating seasonal recipe)

### Effects
The pizza drone lands like a supply pod. Eating:
- Restores health
- Boosts stamina regeneration for a limited time
- Grants a temporary morale bonus (slightly improves reload and revive speeds)

Squads can gather around the box to share slices, creating a lighthearted break between intense firefights.

## Design Principles

1. **Skill Expression**: Movement, combat, and strategy all reward player skill
2. **Teamwork Required**: Some tasks cannot be completed alone
3. **Risk vs. Reward**: Every decision has consequences
4. **Living Universe**: Actions permanently affect the game world
5. **Accessibility**: Clean UI, intuitive controls, forgiving learning curve
6. **Depth**: Hundreds of hours of progression and discovery
